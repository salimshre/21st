## 🔁 Step Definitions

### ✅ Step 1 — Pomodoro Learning Block
```
30 min WORK → 5 min BREAK → repeat
```
- During **work:** single subject, full focus, phone face-down
- During **break:** meditate, do one workout set, or just breathe — NO phone
- After **4 pomodoros (2 hrs):** take a 20-minute long break before the next Study block

### 🍽️ Step 2 — Dinner Meal Block
```
Prep (30 min) → Eat (15 min) → Rest (15 min)
```
- Start a **30-minute countdown** for cooking/prep
- Pack lunchbox if not already done
- Sit and eat properly — no screen, no studying
- 15-minute rest after eating before returning to desk

## 🌙 Pre-Sleep Ritual (22:30 – 23:00)

1. Put all devices on charge in another room or face-down
2. Lie on bed with physical notebook
3. **Read today's subject plan notes** for 10 minutes
4. Write **one sentence** about tomorrow's most important task
5. Lights off by 23:00

> Pre-sleep note reading is scientifically proven to improve overnight memory consolidation. Do not skip this.

## ⚠️ When You Break a Rule

> Punishment systems that add more work create guilt spirals. This system rebuilds momentum instead.

**If you miss or break any block:**

1. **Don't guilt-trip yourself.** One broken block does not ruin the day.
2. Take **5 minutes** to write in your journal:
   - What block did you break?
   - Why did it happen? (honest answer)
   - What will you do differently tomorrow?
3. Continue the schedule from the next block. Do not try to "make up" lost time by skipping breaks.
4. If you break **3+ rules in one day**, do a **15-minute full schedule review** at night and adjust what isn't working.

> A schedule you follow 80% of is better than a perfect schedule you abandon in 3 days.

## 💡 Key Principles

- **Morning = new material** — your brain encodes best before noon
- **Evening = review only** — reinforce what you already learned today
- **Breaks are not optional** — skipping breaks destroys the next work block
- **Rotation beats marathon** — switching subjects every 2 hours improves retention
- **Sleep is study time** — your brain consolidates memory during deep sleep; 7 hrs minimum
